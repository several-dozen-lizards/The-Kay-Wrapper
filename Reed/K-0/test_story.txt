The Three Pigeons of Central Park

Chapter 1: The Characters

Gimpy is a brave one-legged pigeon who is always first to the food. Despite having only one leg, Gimpy navigates the park with remarkable agility and confidence. The other pigeons seem to respect Gimpy's tenacity and boldness.

Patches is a gentle pigeon with unusual brown and white markings. Unlike the typical gray city pigeons, Patches has distinctive coloring that makes her easy to spot. She often stays near the fountain where children throw breadcrumbs.

Squeaker is the youngest of the three, named for his distinctive high-pitched coo. He's still learning the ways of park life and often follows Gimpy around. Squeaker is curious but cautious, watching everything carefully.

Chapter 2: The Daily Routine

Every morning, the three pigeons gather near the east entrance. They have a favorite spot under the old oak tree where the morning sun creates a warm patch. This is where they plan their day's foraging route.

The fountain area is their first stop, usually around 8 AM when the early dog walkers arrive. Many of these humans bring treats specifically for the birds. Patches has learned which humans are the most generous.

By noon, they move to the picnic area where lunch crowds gather. This is the most competitive time, with seagulls and sparrows also competing for scraps. Gimpy's boldness really shines here, darting in between much larger birds to grab choice morsels.

Chapter 3: The Bond

What makes these three pigeons special is their cooperation. While most pigeons compete fiercely, Gimpy, Patches, and Squeaker share their findings. When Patches discovers a generous human, she calls the others over. When Gimpy finds a safe feeding spot, he waits for his friends.

This bond formed during last winter, when food was scarce. Gimpy had found a small protected alcove where heat vents kept the ground warm and snow-free. He brought Patches there, and together they weathered the coldest days. Squeaker joined them in spring, a lost chick they adopted.

The park rangers have noticed this unusual trio. One ranger, Maria, has started documenting their behavior for a wildlife journal. She's amazed by how they communicate and coordinate their movements throughout the day.
